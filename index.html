KANJŌZOKU 環状族

A secretive group of street racers from Japan. Known for their fearless highway runs and underground spirit, they represent freedom, rebellion, and passion for speed.

About

Kanjozoku (カンジョ族) refers to a legendary group of Japanese street racers who ruled the highways of Osaka during the late 1980s to early 2000s. Their name comes from the Kanjo Loop (環状線 - Kanjō-sen), a circular expressway around the city where they performed high-speed runs at night. What made them unique wasn't just their speed — it was their deep passion for street racing, DIY car tuning, and anti-mainstream culture. Kanjozoku cars, often modified Honda Civics, were lightweight, stripped down, and built purely for performance. But Kanjozoku is more than racing — it's about rebellion, identity, and expression. These racers formed a close underground community with their own codes, stickers, masks, and symbols to protect their anonymity and represent their crews.
